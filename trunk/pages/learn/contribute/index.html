Contribute

AusStage welcomes contributions from all users - from artists, spectators, producers, agents, students, teachers, researchers, librarians, archivists and the public.

You can register to add comments and tag records. You can take our training in data entry protocols and procedures to learn how create, edit and copy records. And you can assist AusStage in our ongoing work of editorial review. Even if you're not registered, you can contribute information to AusStage in others ways.

  • Browse, search and send comments. Search for yourself or for a person, organisation or venue you know. Look over the records and contribute information to AusStage by sending a comment. When you are browsing AusStage, you will see an option to Feedback on each record. Click to send feedback on the record you are viewing. When we receive your comment, we will review the record and make appropriate changes. We will let you know when the record is ready to view. 
     
  • Contribute event information. Use this form to contribute information on performing arts productions and other live events. You may contribute information on current, upcoming or past events. You can also use this form to suggest changes to events already in AusStage. When we receive your contribution, we will add information to AusStage where appropriate, and let you know when it is ready to view.
     
  • Contribute resource information. Use this form to contribute information on resources - texts, images, objects, recordings or the like which relate to live performance. You may contribute information about materials in libraries, museums, archives and personal collections. We will add appropriate information to AusStage's resource directory and let you know when it is ready to view.
     
  • Contact us if you have a query or request about information in AusStage. We are able to conduct complex searches on request, undertake detailed analysis and generate reports on the results. We also welcome your suggestions for enhancing the information in AusStage and improving how the database works.

Please include your name and contact details with your contribution. We respect the privacy of personal information. Information is contributed to AusStage under our Terms of Use. Contributions are reviewed for relevance and accuracy before they are added to AusStage.
